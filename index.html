Test Overview

How the IELTS Speaking Test Works

The Speaking test is 11–14 minutes. There are three parts, each testing different speaking skills.

1

Introduction & Interview (4–5 min)

The examiner asks about familiar topics: family, home, studies, hobbies, and daily life. Give 2–3 sentence answers with a reason or example every time.

2

Individual Long Turn (3–4 min)

You get a cue card with a topic. You have 1 minute to prepare notes, then speak for 1–2 minutes. Cover all the bullet points on the card.

3

Two-Way Discussion (4–5 min)

More abstract questions linked to your Part 2 topic. Give extended answers with opinions, reasons, and examples. Aim for 3–5 sentences per answer.

🎯 Target for This Practice

This app targets Band 4.5–5. Focus on complete sentences, giving reasons, and using varied vocabulary.

Part 1 — Introduction & Interview

Choose a Topic

Select one topic. Answer 5 questions on that topic. For each question, speak for at least 30 seconds and give a reason or example.

💡 Part 1 Tips
  • Never say just "Yes" or "No" — always extend your answer
  • Always add a reason: "...because..."
  • Add an example: "For example,..."
  • Speak at a natural, steady pace

Part 2 — Individual Long Turn

Choose a Cue Card

Select a topic. You will have 1 minute to prepare notes, then 2 minutes to speak. Cover all the bullet points on your card.

💡 Part 2 Tips
  • Write key words only during prep time — not full sentences
  • Address every bullet point on the card
  • Keep speaking for the full 2 minutes — add feelings and details!
  • End with a personal comment or feeling about the topic

Part 3 — Two-Way Discussion

Discussion Questions

Answer 4 discussion questions linked to your Part 2 topic. These are more abstract — give opinions about society and ideas. Aim for 3–5 sentences per answer.

💡 Part 3 Tips
  • Give your opinion: "I think... / In my opinion..."
  • Explain why: "This is because... / The main reason is..."
  • Consider both sides: "On the other hand..."
  • Don't be afraid to disagree — just justify your view!
  • Speak for at least 45 seconds per question
